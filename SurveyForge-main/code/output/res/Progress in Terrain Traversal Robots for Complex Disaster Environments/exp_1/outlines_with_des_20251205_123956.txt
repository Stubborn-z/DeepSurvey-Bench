### 4.1 Traversability Assessment Techniques

### 4.2 Dynamic Path Planning Algorithms  
Description: This subsection focuses on path planning algorithms that adapt to real-time changes in the environment, essential for safe navigation through disaster-impacted regions with unpredictable obstacles.
1. Reactive Path Planning: Discussion of reactive strategies that allow robots to dynamically adjust their paths in response to newly detected obstacles, ensuring safe navigation in cluttered environments.
2. Model Predictive Control (MPC): Examination of MPC techniques that utilize predictive models of the terrain to optimize path decisions based on anticipated changes, incorporating constraints related to robot dynamics and environment variability.
3. Sampling-Based Algorithms: Overview of sampling-based algorithms such as RRT* and PRM that facilitate the exploration of high-dimensional configuration spaces to identify safe, efficient paths through complex terrains.

### 4.3 Real-Time Navigation Strategies  
Description: This subsection delves into strategies for achieving effective real-time navigation in disaster environments, integrating localization techniques and environmental mapping improvements to support seamless robot mobility.
1. Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM): Analysis of SLAM techniques that combine mapping the environment with localizing the robot simultaneously to enhance situational awareness and navigational efficiency in feature-poor conditions.
2. Terrain-Aware Navigation: Considerations for navigation strategies that account for terrain properties, allowing robots to make informed movement decisions that reduce the risk of mobility loss or entrapment in challenging areas.
3. Costmap Optimization: Exploration of methods for creating and dynamically updating costmaps that reflect the traversability of regions in real-time, providing robots with a navigational framework that optimizes travel efficiency based on sensor inputs.
